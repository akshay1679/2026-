### What GLBA does

GLBA has **three main parts**:

1. **Financial Privacy Rule**
    
    - Requires banks, lenders, insurance companies, etc. to **explain what customer data they collect and share**
        
    - Customers must be given the **right to opt out** of certain data sharing with non-affiliated third parties
        
2. **Safeguards Rule**
    
    - Requires financial institutions to **protect sensitive customer information**
        
    - They must have a **written security program** (administrative, technical, and physical safeguards)
        
3. **Pretexting Provisions**
    
    - Makes it illegal to obtain customer information through **fraud or impersonation** (aka social engineering)
        

### Who it applies to

Not just banks. GLBA applies to:

- Banks & credit unions
    
- Mortgage lenders & brokers
    
- Investment firms
    
- Insurance companies
    
- Payday lenders
    
- Tax preparers & financial advisors
    
- Any company **significantly engaged in financial activities**
    

### What information is protected

**Nonpublic Personal Information (NPI)**, such as:

- Social Security numbers
    
- Bank account numbers
    
- Credit history
    
- Income and financial records
    

### Why it matters

- Protects consumers from identity theft and data misuse
    
- Forces financial companies to take **data security seriously**
    
- Violations can lead to **fines, lawsuits, and regulatory penalties**
